Holiday Insider Tips

Plan like a local with Dina's favorite advice for navigating New York City's festive season.

1

Best Time to Visit

Weekday mornings around 9–11 AM are the calmest at Rockefeller Center. For the lights, arrive at dusk to watch the city glow.

2

Save on Experiences

Window displays, Bryant Park's market, and most light shows are free. Splurge on cocoa or skating instead of pricey attractions.

3

Stay Warm

Warm up inside Grand Central Terminal, the New York Public Library, or nearby hotel lobbies between outdoor stops.

4

Photo Magic

Shoot the Rockefeller Center tree from the 50th Street side for fewer crowds. Portrait mode keeps the sparkle in focus.

5

Free Entertainment

Listen for carolers at Washington Square Park and free concerts around Lincoln Center and inside Grand Central.

6

Kid-Friendly Route

Combine Rockefeller Center, the LEGO Store, Bryant Park's carousel, and Dylan's Candy Bar for a 90-minute family adventure.

7

Budget Bites

Urbanspace Vanderbilt and Eataly offer delicious, affordable meals. Food carts serve $3 pretzels and cocoa.

8

Transit Tricks

Use the B, D, F, or M trains to the 47-50 Sts-Rockefeller Center station. Kids under 44 inches ride free, and unlimited MetroCards pay off fast.
